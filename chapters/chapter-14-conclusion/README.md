## Chapter Overview

The final chapter steps back from implementation to survey the generative AI landscape: a timeline from the VAE/GAN era through transformers to today's large multimodal models, the current state of LLMs, text-to-code, and text-to-image systems, and thoughtful consideration of the future and ethics. You will synthesize everything learned across 14 chapters, plan your capstone multimodal project, and engage critically with bias, misinformation, copyright, and environmental costs of generative AI.

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

1. Trace a timeline of generative AI from 2014 VAEs/GANs through transformers to diffusion and LLMs
2. Summarize the current state of large language models, text-to-code, and text-to-image systems
3. Evaluate generative AI applications in everyday life, the workplace, and education
4. Analyze ethical challenges: bias, deepfakes, copyright, labor impact, and environmental cost
5. Synthesize the six generative method families and when to choose each
6. Define a capstone project plan integrating course techniques with ethical guardrails
7. Articulate informed predictions for the future direction of generative AI research
8. Apply modern LLM engineering patterns: RAG, PEFT, preference optimization, inference optimization, and evaluation harnesses
